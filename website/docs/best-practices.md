# Best Practices & Tips

Getting the most out of SyncVRC isn't just about technical setup; it's also about how you interact with the AI. Here are the best practices to ensure smooth, accurate, and fast translations in VRChat.

## 1. Speaking for the AI (Speech-to-Text)
The Faster-Whisper model is powerful, but it still relies on clear audio input to understand you correctly.
* **Enunciate Clearly:** Mumbling, whispering, or speaking too softly can cause the AI to guess words incorrectly or miss sentences entirely.
* **Pacing and Pauses:** Cloud APIs translate text sentence by sentence. If you speak in one massive, endless run-on sentence, the AI will wait until you finally stop before it sends the audio for translation. Take natural breaths and pause slightly at the end of your sentences to trigger the translation faster.
* **Avoid Background Noise:** While the AI filters some noise, loud background music, desk pounding, or a fan blowing directly into your physical microphone will degrade transcription accuracy.

## 2. Optimizing the Push-to-Talk Workflow
If you are using a Free Tier API or playing in a chaotic public instance, the **Push to Record** mode is your best friend.
* Press and hold the button, speak your complete thought, and then release it. This gives the AI a perfectly packaged audio clip to process, saving your API quota and preventing embarrassing "hot-mic" moments where the AI translates your background conversations.

## 3. Handling VRChat Slang and Context
* **Keep it Standard:** The Cloud AI models are trained on standard languages. Heavy internet slang, highly specific VRChat lore, or mixed-language sentences (e.g., "Japanglish") might confuse the translator. 
* **Keep it Simple:** When in doubt, use simpler sentence structures. The phrase "I am going to another world" translates much more reliably than "I'm gonna dip and portal hop."

## 4. VRChat Etiquette
* **Set Expectations:** It is highly recommended to put "(Translator)" or "(AI Voice)" in your VRChat bio or status bubble. This sets expectations for other players that there might be a 1-2 second delay in your responses, making conversations flow much more naturally.
